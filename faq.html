Tile & Stone Cleaning FAQ

How much does tile and grout cleaning cost?

The cost depends on several factors including the total area, type of surface, level of soiling, and whether additional services like sealing are required. As a general guide, tile and grout cleaning starts from $25 per square metre. Bathrooms typically cost up to $200 per sqm, kitchens range from $80 to $200 per sqm, and full house floors range from $200 to $400 per sqm. We provide free, no-obligation quotes after inspecting the area.

How to clean white grout and tile?

White grout is particularly prone to discolouration from dirt, mould, and spills. For routine maintenance, use a pH-neutral cleaner and a soft brush. However, for deep staining, professional cleaning with hot water extraction and rotary scrubbing is the most effective solution. We can also apply grout colour sealing after cleaning to keep white grout looking fresh for years.

What is the best cleaner for natural stone tiles?

Natural stone tiles such as marble, travertine, and limestone require pH-neutral cleaners specifically formulated for stone. Avoid anything acidic (vinegar, citrus-based products) or alkaline (bleach, ammonia). These can etch, discolour, or damage the stone surface. For professional results, we use specialised stone cleaning solutions paired with appropriate equipment for each stone type.

How to deep clean tile floors?

Deep cleaning tile floors requires more than a mop and bucket. The most effective method combines a professional-grade alkaline cleaning solution with rotary scrubbing to break down embedded dirt and grime, followed by hot water extraction to remove all residue. This process reaches deep into grout lines and tile pores where regular mopping cannot. We recommend professional deep cleaning every 12 to 18 months.

Is vinegar safe to use on tile and grout?

While vinegar is often suggested as a natural cleaning solution, it is acidic and can damage certain surfaces over time. It is generally safe for ceramic and porcelain tiles but should never be used on natural stone (marble, travertine, limestone, slate) as the acid will etch and dull the surface. It can also weaken grout over time with repeated use. We recommend pH-neutral cleaners for all tile and stone surfaces.

How often should I have my tiles professionally cleaned?

For most residential settings, professional tile and grout cleaning every 12 to 18 months is ideal. High-traffic areas like kitchen floors and entryways may benefit from annual cleaning. Commercial spaces typically need quarterly or bi-annual professional cleaning depending on foot traffic. Regular professional cleaning prevents permanent staining and extends the life of your grout and sealant.

What is the difference between sealing and polishing?

Sealing is a protective treatment that prevents stains and moisture from penetrating the stone or grout. It does not change the appearance of the surface. Polishing is a finishing process that uses diamond abrasives to create a smooth, reflective surface on natural stone. Many surfaces benefit from both — polishing first for the desired finish, then sealing for long-term protection.
