Professional Ceramic Tile & Grout Cleaning

Tile and Stone Cleaners provides professional ceramic tile and grout cleaning services for both residential and commercial properties across Sydney. Whether you have glazed or unglazed ceramic tiles, our technicians use the right techniques and products to achieve a deep, thorough clean without damaging your surfaces.

Ceramic tiles are found in nearly every Australian home -- in kitchens, bathrooms, laundries, and living areas. While ceramic is one of the most durable and low-maintenance flooring options available, proper cleaning and grout care are essential to maintain its appearance over time.

What is Ceramic?

Ceramic tiles are man-made tiles produced by shaping and firing clay at high temperatures. The resulting tile is porous by nature and is typically sealed with a glaze to create a protective, decorative surface.

Glazed ceramic tiles have a glass-like coating applied after the initial firing. The glaze provides colour, pattern, and a protective barrier that ranges from high gloss to matte finishes. Because the colour is applied as a surface coating, glazed tiles have a different colour on the surface than the body beneath.

Unglazed ceramic tiles have colour that runs throughout the entire body of the tile. They are harder and more scratch-resistant than glazed tiles but are also more challenging to clean because dirt can penetrate the porous surface more easily.

Benefits of Glazed Ceramic

Glazed ceramic tiles offer numerous practical advantages that make them one of the most popular flooring choices for Australian homes and businesses.

  • Exceptionally long-lasting and durable when properly maintained
  • Easy to clean with basic household products
  • Does not harbour odours, allergens, or bacteria on the glazed surface
  • Scratch and moisture resistant
  • Non-combustible and fire resistant
  • Available in a vast range of colours, patterns, and sizes

Cleaning Challenges

Despite their beauty and practicality, ceramic tiles require proper cleaning techniques to maintain their appearance. The glazed surface itself is relatively easy to keep clean, but grout lines are porous and absorb dirt, moisture, and staining agents over time.

Unglazed ceramic tiles present additional challenges. Without the protective glaze layer, dirt and grime can penetrate the tile's pores, making surface-level mopping insufficient. Professional deep cleaning with appropriate equipment is often needed to extract embedded contaminants from unglazed ceramic.

TACT Cleaning Principles

Professional ceramic tile cleaning follows the TACT principle -- four interconnected factors that determine cleaning effectiveness. Adjusting any one factor affects the others.

T

Temperature

Higher water temperature increases cleaning effectiveness by improving chemical activity and loosening bonds between dirt and the surface.

A

Agitation

Mechanical action -- scrubbing, brushing, or machine agitation -- physically breaks the bond between contaminants and the tile or grout surface.

C

Chemicals

The right cleaning agent for the specific soil type. pH-neutral for maintenance, alkaline for grease, and specialised products for stubborn stains.

T

Time

Dwell time allows cleaning chemicals to penetrate and break down contaminants before extraction. Rushing reduces effectiveness significantly.

Sealing Ceramic Tiles

Glazed ceramic tiles generally do not require sealing -- the glaze itself acts as a protective barrier. However, the grout between tiles absolutely does need sealing, as grout is porous and will absorb moisture, dirt, and staining agents without protection.

Unglazed and porous ceramic tiles benefit significantly from water-based impregnating sealers. These sealers fill the micro-pores in the tile body, creating an invisible barrier that repels water and oil-based contaminants while allowing the tile to breathe naturally.

Ceramic Tile Care

Proper daily care keeps your ceramic tiles looking their best and extends the life of your grout between professional services.

Do's

  • Sweep or vacuum daily to remove grit and debris that scratches the surface
  • Place mats at every entrance to trap sand and dirt
  • Ventilate bathrooms and showers after use to reduce moisture buildup
  • Use caulk at corners and transitions instead of grout
  • Mop 2-3 times per week with 1 cap of dishwashing liquid per 3 litres of hot water
  • Clean spills promptly to prevent grout staining

Don'ts

  • Don't use abrasive cleaners or scouring pads on glazed surfaces
  • Avoid excessive water that pools in grout lines
  • Never use oil-based cleaners that leave residue
  • Don't neglect grout sealing -- it is the most vulnerable part of the installation
  • Avoid harsh chemicals that can strip grout sealers

Grout Cleaning Methods

Grout is the most challenging part of any ceramic tile installation to keep clean. Over time, grout absorbs dirt, moisture, and staining agents that regular mopping cannot remove. Here are two effective DIY grout cleaning methods for maintenance between professional services.

Bleach Solution Method

Mix bleach and water in a 50/50 ratio. Apply to grout lines and allow to sit for 5-10 minutes. Scrub with a stiff brush, then rinse thoroughly with clean water. Always ensure adequate ventilation when using bleach, wear protective gloves, and rinse the surface completely after cleaning.

Safety Warning: Never mix bleach with other cleaning substances. Combining bleach with ammonia, vinegar, or other chemicals creates toxic fumes that are extremely dangerous. Only use bleach on its own, diluted with water, in a well-ventilated area.

Hydrogen Peroxide Alternative

For those sensitive to chemical cleaning products, hydrogen peroxide is an excellent odourless alternative for grout cleaning. Apply hydrogen peroxide directly to grout lines, allow to sit for 10-15 minutes, then scrub and rinse. It is effective against mould, mildew, and general discolouration without the strong fumes associated with bleach.
