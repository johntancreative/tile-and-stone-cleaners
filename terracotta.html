What is Terracotta?

The word "terracotta" literally translates to "baked earth" in Italian. Terracotta tiles are made from natural clay that is shaped and fired at relatively low temperatures, producing the distinctive warm, reddish-brown colour that the material is known for. Each tile has subtle variations in colour and texture, giving terracotta its unique, handcrafted character that is difficult to replicate with manufactured alternatives.

Terracotta has been used for thousands of years and remains a popular choice for its old-world, Mediterranean and rustic styling. It is one of the most versatile tile materials, suitable for both indoor and outdoor applications including kitchens, hallways, patios, and pool surrounds. Unlike many other ceramic tiles, terracotta feels warmer underfoot, making it a comfortable choice for living areas.

The natural variation in each terracotta tile means no two floors are exactly alike. This unique appearance, combined with its warmth and durability, makes terracotta a sought-after material for homeowners who want a floor with genuine character and history.

Sealing Requirements

Raw terracotta clay is highly absorbent and porous, which means it stains easily if left unsealed. Spills from coffee, wine, oil, and other common household liquids can penetrate the tile surface within minutes, leaving permanent marks that are extremely difficult to remove without professional intervention.

A deep-penetrating impregnating sealer is recommended for terracotta tiles. This type of sealer soaks into the pores of the clay and creates an invisible barrier that repels moisture and stains from within, while still allowing the tile to breathe naturally. Sealing is essential both for new terracotta installations and for maintaining existing floors.

When cleaning terracotta tiles, never use pressure above 1,100 PSI. Terracotta is softer than porcelain or granite, and excessive pressure can erode the surface, blast out grout, and cause irreversible damage to the tile face. Always use low-pressure equipment with appropriate cleaning solutions designed for natural clay tiles.

Sealing Options for Terracotta

Choosing the right sealer for your terracotta tiles depends on the desired finish, the tile's location, and the level of protection required. There are two main categories of sealers used on terracotta.

Impregnating Sealer

  • Uses nano-technology to penetrate deep into the clay's pores for lasting protection
  • Breathable -- allows moisture to escape from within the tile naturally
  • Available in transparent finish that preserves the natural terracotta appearance
  • Also available in colour-enhancing finish that deepens and enriches the reddish-brown tones
  • Long-lasting protection that doesn't require frequent reapplication
  • Shields against both water and oil-based stains
Recommended

Topical Sealer

  • Creates a protective coating on the surface of the tile
  • Available in gloss, satin, or matte finish options to suit different aesthetics
  • Approximate lifespan of 3 years before reapplication is needed
  • Enhances the appearance of the tile with a visible sheen
  • Provides a physical barrier against spills and foot traffic wear
  • May require stripping before reapplication to prevent buildup

Porosity Test: To determine whether your terracotta tiles need sealing, perform a simple water drop test. Place a few drops of water on the tile surface and observe how quickly it absorbs. If the water soaks in within 1-2 minutes and darkens the tile, the surface is porous and requires sealing. If the water beads up and sits on the surface, the existing sealer is still providing adequate protection. Perform this test in multiple areas, as wear patterns can vary across the floor.

Stripping & Sealing Terracotta

Over time, terracotta tiles can lose their beauty as old sealant degrades, foot traffic wears down the protective coating, and dirt becomes embedded in the tile's porous surface. When this happens, the tiles typically need to be stripped back to bare clay and resealed -- a process that should be carried out every 2-3 years depending on the level of foot traffic and exposure.

At Tile and Stone Cleaners, we use advanced machinery and professional-grade water-based sealers to strip and reseal terracotta floors. Our process involves removing all existing sealant and buildup, deep cleaning the tile and grout, allowing the surface to dry completely, and then applying fresh sealer in multiple coats for lasting protection.

We exclusively use water-based sealers that are low-VOC and environmentally responsible. These sealers penetrate effectively, dry quickly, and produce minimal odour during application -- making them safe for use in occupied homes and businesses.

Stripping & Sealing

Cleaning & Sealing

Professional deep clean of terracotta tiles and grout followed by protective sealer application. Ideal for floors where the existing sealer is worn but doesn't require full stripping -- restores the warm, natural appearance of the clay.

Tile & Grout Cleaning

Specialised terracotta tile and grout cleaning using professional-grade equipment calibrated for soft natural clay. We extract embedded dirt, grime, and discolouration from both tile faces and grout lines without damaging the delicate surface.

4

Tile Sealing

Seal porous terracotta tiles to prevent staining and moisture damage. Our impregnating sealers create a protective barrier within the clay that repels water and oil-based contaminants while preserving the tile's natural appearance and allowing it to breathe.

5

Stripping

Remove old sealant, wax buildup, and surface residue to restore your terracotta tiles to their original bare clay state. Proper stripping is essential before any new sealer application to ensure adhesion, even coverage, and optimal penetration.

Remove unsightly white salt deposits that appear on terracotta tile surfaces. Efflorescence is common on terracotta due to its porous nature -- we use specialised chemicals to dissolve and extract these deposits without damaging the underlying clay.
