What is Sandstone?

Sandstone is a sedimentary rock composed of small, sand-sized particles that have been compressed and cemented together over millions of years. The primary minerals in sandstone are quartz and feldspar, with varying amounts of calcite, gypsum, and iron compounds that give the stone its characteristic colours.

Sandstone is found in a wide range of natural colours including tan, brown, yellow, red, grey, pink, and white. The colour variation depends on the mineral composition and the conditions under which the stone formed. This natural colour diversity makes sandstone a versatile and attractive choice for both indoor and outdoor applications.

Cleaning Sandstone

Sandstone is susceptible to scratches, stains, and cracking, making proper cleaning techniques essential for preserving the stone's appearance and structural integrity. Unlike harder natural stones, sandstone requires a gentler approach to avoid surface damage.

Avoid synthetic sealers on sandstone -- instead, use advanced water-based sealers that allow the stone to breathe while providing effective protection against staining and moisture. Synthetic coatings can trap moisture within the stone, leading to spalling, flaking, and accelerated deterioration.

Older buildings often feature chalky, porous sandstone varieties that are even more susceptible to staining than marble. These softer sandstones absorb liquids quickly and can be permanently damaged by incorrect cleaning products.

When cleaning sandstone, only use neutral pH detergents. Never pressure clean sandstone above 800 PSI, and only if the grout is in good condition. Excessive pressure can erode the stone's surface, blast out grout, and cause damage that is expensive to repair.

Sandstone looks best with a honed or low-to-medium sheen finish. High-polish finishes are difficult to achieve on most sandstone varieties due to the stone's granular structure, and a honed finish provides a more natural, authentic appearance.

Sealing Sandstone

Sealing is critical for protecting sandstone from staining and moisture damage. Honed sandstone finishes are more permeable than polished surfaces, meaning they absorb liquids faster and require more robust sealer protection.

Impregnating Sealer

  • Penetrates deep into the stone's pores for lasting protection
  • Uses nano-technology to create an invisible barrier within the stone
  • Available in transparent or colour-enhancing finish options
  • Shields against both water and oil-based stains
  • Allows the stone to breathe naturally, preventing moisture entrapment
  • Ideal for sandstone's porous structure
Recommended

Topical Sealer

  • Creates a protective coating on the surface of the stone
  • Available in gloss, satin, or matte finish options
  • Provides surface-level protection against foot traffic and spills
  • Does not penetrate like an impregnating sealer
  • Approximate lifespan of 3 years before reapplication is needed
  • Requires stripping before reapplication

Pre-Sealing Preparation

Proper preparation before sealing sandstone is critical. Rushing this step leads to trapped stains, sealer failure, and costly rework.

1

Clean the Surface

Thoroughly clean all sandstone surfaces and achieve the desired condition. Stains sealed over may become permanent and impossible to remove later.

2

Avoid Acid Cleaners

Never use acidic cleaners on sandstone. Acid causes irreversible etching and chemical damage to the calcite content in the stone.

3

Remove Old Coatings

Strip and remove any existing coatings, wax, or old sealers before applying new sealer. Old layers prevent proper adhesion and penetration.

4

Ensure Complete Dryness

The sandstone must be completely dry before sealing. Moisture trapped beneath sealer causes white hazing, peeling, and premature failure.

Protection from Acid Etching

Sandstone contains calcite, making it susceptible to acid etching -- the same vulnerability shared by limestone, marble, and travertine. When acidic substances come into contact with the calcite in sandstone, a chemical reaction dissolves the surface layer and leaves dull, rough marks.

Common household substances that damage sandstone include lemon juice, cola, wine, and vinegar. Honed finishes hide etch marks better than polished surfaces because the matte texture blends minor damage more naturally.

Acid Warning: Always clean acidic spills on sandstone immediately. Lemon juice, wine, cola, and vinegar will etch and permanently damage sandstone surfaces on contact. Keep acidic cleaning products away from all calcite-based natural stones. If etching has already occurred, professional honing may be required to restore the surface.

Sandstone Care & Maintenance

Proper daily care extends the life of your sandstone surfaces and reduces the frequency of professional restoration.

Do's

  • Clean spills immediately to prevent staining and etching
  • Seal sandstone with appropriate stone-specific sealers
  • Place mats at every entrance to trap grit and sand
  • Use a microfiber mop for regular cleaning
  • Clean regularly with neutral pH stone-safe detergents
  • Schedule professional cleaning every 1-2 years

Don'ts

  • Never use bleach, ammonia, or vinegar on sandstone
  • Don't use acidic cleaners of any kind on the surface
  • Never use abrasive cleaners or scouring pads
  • Don't use synthetic sealers or urethane coatings
  • Avoid pressure cleaning above 800 PSI

Our Sandstone Services

We offer a comprehensive range of sandstone cleaning, restoration, and protection services. Every service is tailored to your stone's specific condition and requirements.

1

Stripping & Sealing

Complete strip, deep clean, and reseal of sandstone surfaces in kitchens, bathrooms, outdoor areas, and commercial spaces. We remove all existing coatings before applying fresh, long-lasting sealer protection.

2

Cleaning & Sealing

For dulled sandstone surfaces, new installations, or worn sealant. We restore the natural warmth and character of your sandstone before applying protective sealer to guard against future damage and staining.

3

Tile Sealing

Seal porous sandstone tiles to prevent staining and moisture damage. Our sealers create a protective barrier within the stone that repels water and oil-based contaminants while allowing the stone to breathe naturally.

4

Stripping

Remove old sealant, wax buildup, and surface residue to restore your sandstone's original appearance. Proper stripping is essential before any new sealer application to ensure adhesion and even coverage.

5

Grinding

Smooth out surface imperfections and open a new layer of stone for a honed finish. Grinding removes deep scratches, stubborn stains, and corrects lippage -- uneven tile edges caused by shifting or poor installation.

6

Honing

Achieve a smooth, satin-feel sandstone surface. Honed finishes are the most popular choice for sandstone and require less maintenance than polished surfaces while providing a natural, authentic look.

7

Polishing

Where the stone type allows, fine-grade diamond discs create a low-to-medium sheen finish on sandstone. Polishing removes surface scratches and enhances the stone's natural colour and grain patterns.

8

Efflorescence Treatment

Remove unsightly salt deposit buildup that appears as white powdery residue on sandstone surfaces. We use specialised equipment and chemicals to dissolve and extract efflorescence without damaging the underlying stone.

9

Minor Tile Repair

Fix cracked, chipped, loose, and discoloured sandstone tiles in both indoor and outdoor settings. Minor repairs prevent further damage and extend the lifespan of your sandstone installation.

10

Grout Colour Sealing

Re-colour stained, discoloured grout lines or alter the existing grout colour to refresh the overall appearance of your sandstone floor. Colour sealing provides a uniform look while protecting grout from future staining.
