What is Granite?

Granite is a natural igneous rock formed from molten lava (magma) that cooled slowly deep beneath the earth's surface, never reaching the surface during its formation. This slow cooling process allowed large mineral crystals to form, giving granite its distinctive speckled appearance and exceptional hardness.

Granite ranks 7 on the Mohs hardness scale, making it one of the hardest natural stones used in construction. It is composed primarily of silica (quartz), along with feldspar, mica, and other minerals that give each variety its unique colour and pattern. This mineral composition makes granite highly resistant to scratching, heat, and most chemicals.

Granite is suitable for a wide range of applications including flooring, kitchen and bathroom countertops, wall coverings, outdoor paving, and commercial surfaces. Its natural beauty, combined with its extraordinary durability, has made it one of the most popular natural stones for both residential and commercial projects across Sydney.

Popular Granite Colours

Granite comes in an enormous range of natural colours and patterns. Here are three of the most popular granite varieties used in Sydney homes and commercial spaces.

1

Absolute Black

A pure, deep black granite with a uniform colour and minimal grain variation. Creates a sleek, contemporary look and is popular for kitchen countertops, bathroom vanities, and modern flooring.

2

Black Galaxy

A striking black granite featuring distinctive gold and copper flecks throughout the surface. The metallic speckles are caused by bronzite mineral inclusions, giving the stone a dramatic, star-filled appearance.

3

Kashmir White

A light white granite with scattered speckles of grey, garnet, and dark mineral inclusions. Offers a bright, elegant appearance that works well in both traditional and modern interior designs.

Sealing Granite

Despite its exceptional hardness, granite is still a porous natural stone that benefits from sealing. Sealing creates a protective barrier that prevents stains from penetrating the stone's surface and makes ongoing cleaning significantly easier.

Impregnating Sealer

  • Soaks deep into the stone's pores and reacts chemically for lasting protection
  • Available in transparent finish that preserves the granite's natural appearance
  • Colour-enhancing option available to deepen and enrich the stone's natural tones
  • Does not alter the surface texture or create a visible coating
  • Shields against both water and oil-based stain penetration
  • Long-lasting protection with less frequent reapplication than topical sealers
Recommended

Topical Sealer

  • Creates a protective coating on the surface of the stone
  • Available in gloss, satin, or matte finish options
  • Protects from external factors including foot traffic and spills
  • Approximate lifespan of 3 years before reapplication is needed
  • May require stripping before reapplication to prevent buildup
  • Can enhance the stone's colour and add visible sheen

Granite Care Tips

Proper daily care keeps your granite surfaces looking pristine and extends the lifespan of your sealer. Follow these guidelines to protect your granite investment between professional services.

Do's

  • Dust and sweep regularly to remove grit and particles that can scratch the surface
  • Use stone-specific care products designed for natural granite
  • Clean with a neutral pH cleaner -- avoid anything acidic or highly alkaline
  • Place coasters under glasses and cups, especially those containing acidic drinks
  • Use soft brush vacuum attachments when cleaning granite floors
  • Wipe spills immediately to prevent potential staining

Don'ts

  • Never use vinegar, Windex, or common household cleaners on granite
  • Never mix bleach and ammonia -- this creates toxic fumes and can damage the stone
  • Avoid scratching powders and abrasive cleaners that dull the polish
  • Don't drag heavy objects across granite surfaces -- always lift
  • Never use Soft Scrub or similar cream cleansers -- they contain abrasives
  • Avoid acidic cleaning products including lemon-based and citrus cleaners

Care Warning: Avoid vinegar, Soft Scrub, Windex, and all acidic cleaners on granite surfaces. These products can etch, dull, and permanently damage the stone's polished finish. Never mix bleach and ammonia -- this creates toxic chloramine gas that is dangerous to inhale. When vacuuming granite floors, always use soft brush attachments to prevent scratching. If you are unsure whether a product is safe for granite, contact a professional before use.

Our Granite Services

We offer a comprehensive range of granite cleaning, restoration, and protection services. Every service is tailored to your stone's specific condition and requirements.

1

Stripping & Sealing

Complete strip, deep clean, and reseal of granite surfaces. We remove all existing coatings, old sealant, and buildup before applying fresh, long-lasting sealer protection to restore and safeguard your granite.

2

Cleaning & Sealing

Professional deep clean of granite surfaces followed by protective sealer application. Ideal for granite that has lost its lustre, new installations, or surfaces with worn sealant that need refreshing.

3

Tile & Grout Cleaning

Specialised granite tile and grout cleaning using professional-grade equipment. We extract embedded dirt, grime, and discolouration from both tile faces and grout lines without damaging the stone.

4

Tile Sealing

Seal porous granite tiles to prevent staining and moisture damage. Our impregnating sealers create a protective barrier within the stone that repels water and oil-based contaminants while preserving the natural appearance.

5

Stripping

Remove old sealant, wax buildup, and surface residue to restore your granite's original appearance. Proper stripping prepares the surface for fresh sealer application with optimal adhesion and penetration.

6

Grinding

Smooth out surface imperfections, remove deep scratches, and correct lippage -- uneven tile edges caused by shifting or poor installation. Diamond grinding opens a fresh layer of stone for honing or polishing.

7

Honing

Achieve a satin-smooth, velvety granite surface with a low-sheen matte finish. Honed granite requires less maintenance than polished surfaces, shows fewer marks, and suits casual and contemporary environments.

8

Polishing

Fine-grade diamond discs create a reflective, mirror-like finish on your granite. Polishing reveals the stone's natural crystal structure and mineral colours, producing a smooth surface that collects less dirt.

9

Efflorescence Treatment

Remove unsightly white salt deposit buildup that appears as powdery residue on granite surfaces. We use specialised equipment and chemicals to dissolve and extract efflorescence without damaging the stone.

10

Minor Tile Repair

Fix cracked, chipped, loose, and discoloured granite tiles in both indoor and outdoor settings. Minor repairs prevent further damage and extend the lifespan of your granite installation.

11

Grout Colour Sealing

Re-colour stained, discoloured grout lines or change the existing grout colour to refresh the overall appearance of your granite floor. Colour sealing provides a uniform look while simultaneously protecting grout from future staining and moisture damage.
