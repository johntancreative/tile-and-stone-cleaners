What is Slate?

Slate is a fine-grained metamorphic rock formed from the compression of clay and volcanic ash over millions of years. It is available in a range of natural colours including grey, black, red, and purple, with each piece displaying unique patterns and colour variations.

The stone's naturally uneven, cleft surface provides excellent slip resistance, making it popular for both indoor and outdoor applications. Slate is commonly used for flooring, wall cladding, roofing, outdoor patios, garden paths, and feature walls.

While slate is dense, it is also highly porous and requires quality sealing to prevent staining and moisture absorption. Oil-repellent impregnating sealers are recommended for slate to provide comprehensive protection against both water and oil-based contaminants.

Cleaning Slate

The approach to cleaning slate varies depending on the type of slate and any existing sealers on the surface. Before any cleaning begins, we always test the surface to determine the optimal approach and avoid potential damage.

Alkaline cleaning products are safe for use on slate and are our preferred choice for professional cleaning. Slate does not contain calcite like limestone or travertine, so it is naturally resistant to acid etching. However, we still recommend avoiding acidic cleaners as they can damage sealers and affect the stone's appearance.

When cleaning slate, it is important to remove any existing sealer before applying cleaning solutions to ensure the products can properly reach the stone's surface. Old, deteriorated sealers can trap dirt and prevent effective cleaning.

Caution: Avoid high-pressure cleaning on slate surfaces with uneven textures and compromised grout. The uneven surface of cleft slate creates gaps where high-pressure water can penetrate beneath tiles, loosen adhesive, and blast out grout. Always assess grout condition before pressure cleaning slate.

Sealing Slate

Due to slate's high porosity, quality sealing is essential for long-term protection. Without proper sealing, slate absorbs liquids quickly and can stain permanently. There are two main types of sealers used on slate surfaces.

Impregnating Sealer

  • Uses nano-technology to penetrate deep into the stone's pores
  • Creates a breathable, invisible barrier within the stone
  • Repels both water and oil-based stains
  • Does not alter the stone's natural appearance or texture
  • Long-lasting protection that does not peel or flake
  • Ideal for slate's highly porous structure
Recommended

Topical Sealer

  • Approximate lifespan of 3 years before reapplication
  • Available in high-gloss option for enhanced appearance
  • Creates a protective coating on the surface
  • Enhances colour and provides a wet-look finish when desired
  • Requires stripping before reapplication to prevent buildup

Slate Maintenance

Despite being resistant to acid etching (due to the absence of calcite), slate's high porosity means it requires quality sealing and ongoing maintenance to keep it looking its best.

Grout lines in slate installations often require additional scrubbing attention. The uneven surface of cleft slate creates deeper grout joints that collect more dirt and grime than flat-surface tiles. Regular grout cleaning helps maintain a clean, uniform appearance.

Testing Tip: Always test cleaning products on a small, inconspicuous area of slate before applying them to the full surface. Different slate types can react differently to cleaning chemicals, and testing ensures you won't cause unintended discolouration or damage to the stone or existing sealer.

Tile Sealing
